Brain Conditions

What is Stroke?

Stroke is a medical condition that occurs when blood supply to the brain is interrupted or reduced. This can be caused by a blood clot (ischemic stroke) or bleeding (hemorrhagic stroke).Â 

Types
There are many different types of brain stroke, each with its own characteristics . Some common types include:
Ischemic stroke: This is the most common type of stroke and occurs when a blood clot blocks blood flow to the brain. Hemorrhagic stroke: This occurs when a blood vessel in the brain bursts and bleeds.

Risk Factors:
High blood pressure
High cholesterol
Heart disease
Diabetes Obesity Family history of stroke Age (the risk increases with age)

Treatment
Ischemic stroke: Treatment often involves medications to dissolve the blood clot and restore blood flow to the brain. In some cases, surgery may be necessary to remove the clot.
Hemorrhagic stroke: Treatment often involves medications to control bleeding and reduce brain pressure. Surgery may also be necessary to repair the damaged blood vessel.
